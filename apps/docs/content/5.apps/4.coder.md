# Coder

::note
Every step below is captured from a live end-to-end run on the local stack — the screenshots refresh on each capture, so this guide cannot drift from the real product.
::

## Sign in to Coder

Coder is where a software project lives: its vision, the repos it touches, its team and its user stories. Like every OpenApe app you sign in with your email domain and a passkey — no new account, no password.

### Open Coder

The start page is the sign-in: enter your email and continue.

![Open Coder](/guides/coder/sign-in/01-landing.png)

### Signed in — your projects

On your first visit the list is empty. You only ever see projects you belong to — nobody outside a project can tell it, or its people, exist.

![Signed in — your projects](/guides/coder/sign-in/02-home-empty.png)

## Create a project

A project is the home for one software effort. You create it, become its admin, and give it a vision and the repos it touches — the shared context everyone on the team reads.

### Start a new project

Click **New project** and name it. As its creator you become the project admin.

![Start a new project](/guides/coder/create-project/01-dialog.png)

### Name it

The project page opens: an overview with its vision and repos, a members panel, and the story board.

![Name it](/guides/coder/create-project/02-project-page.png)

### Vision and repos

The vision is plain prose; the repos are full URLs to the code it touches — GitHub, GitLab, Forgejo or self-hosted, never just `owner/repo`. Both stay editable as the project grows.

![Vision and repos](/guides/coder/create-project/03-scope-saved.png)

## Add a user story

User stories live in the project, where everyone can see them — not in mail threads. A story has the usual parts and can carry repos, links, test references and a status.

### Open the story board

The board shows every story in the project at a glance. It is empty for a new project.

![Open the story board](/guides/coder/add-story/01-board-empty.png)

### Write the story

Give it a title and the story sentence — *As … I want … so that …*. The optional repos, links and test references can be added afterwards.

![Write the story](/guides/coder/add-story/02-story-dialog.png)

### Story created

The story opens, laid out for reading — sentence, criteria and any references — no files or code to open.

![Story created](/guides/coder/add-story/03-story-detail.png)

## Browse the board

Back on the board, every story shows its status. Group or filter to see exactly where the project stands.

### All stories with status

Each story carries a status badge. The board groups them so you read the project state in one look.

![All stories with status](/guides/coder/story-board/01-board.png)

## Invite the team

A project admin adds people by email and controls, per member, what they may do. No email is sent — a new member is added read-only and sees it in their inbox at the next sign-in. Every write capability is granted explicitly.

### Open the members panel

The Members panel lists who belongs to the project and their role.

![Open the members panel](/guides/coder/invite-members/01-members.png)

### Add by email

Add a person by email address. The app never reveals whether that address already has an OpenApe identity — and only a human admin, never an agent, can do it.

![Add by email](/guides/coder/invite-members/02-invite-dialog.png)

### They'll see it in their inbox

No email is sent: the new member is added as read-only and learns of it from an inbox notification the next time they sign in. The optional button drafts a heads-up in your own mail client — nothing leaves through our server.

![They'll see it in their inbox](/guides/coder/invite-members/03-member-added.png)
